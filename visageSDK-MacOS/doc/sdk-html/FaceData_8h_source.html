290  /* Translation of the head, compensated.
291  * <i>This variable is set only while tracker is tracking (TRACK_STAT_OK).</i>
292  *
293  * Translation is expressed with three coordinates x, y, z.
294  * The coordinate system is such that when looking towards the camera, the direction of x is to the
295  * left, y iz up, and z points towards the viewer. The global origin (0,0,0) is placed at the camera.
296  *
297  * Translation of the head relative to its position in the first video frame.
298  * This is compensated translation estimated by the tracker.
299  * Compensated translation values take into account the fact that the tracker
300  * uses a relatively flat face model for tracking, so the center of rotation of this model
301  * is in the front area of the head, while the anatomical center
302  * of rotation is behind, in the base of the neck. Therefore, when the rotation
303  * is applied to a 3D head model with anatomically correct center of rotation, the
304  * face naturally translates as well. When this translation is compounded with
305  * the translation values obtained from the tracker, the total resulting translation
306  * is exaggerated. To avoid this exaggerated translation of the animated head,
307  * the translation can be compensated. The compensation algorithm
308  * estimates how much the translation would be exaggerated, and makes it that much
309  * smaller. The compensated translation can directly be applied to animated head
310  * models that use the neck base as the center of rotation, and is expected to give
311  * better results than the uncompensated translation.
312  *
313  * @see faceTranslation, faceRotation
314  */
315  float faceTranslationCompensated[3];
